## Transformer Attention Mechanisms: A Comparative Analysis ### Self-Attention Self-attention is the foundational mechanism in the Transformer architecture. For each position in the input sequence, it computes three vectors: query (Q), key (K), and value (V) through learned linear projections. The attention score between positions i and j is computed as the dot product of Q_i and K_j, scaled by √d_k, and passed through a softmax to obtain attention weights. ### Cross-Attention Cross-attention extends the self-attention mechanism to operate between two different sequences. In the Transformer decoder, queries are derived from the decoder's output while keys and values come from the encoder's representation. This enables the model to attend to relevant parts of the input sequence while generating output. ### Sparse Attention Sparse attention patterns reduce the quadratic complexity of standard attention by limiting which positions can attend to each other. The Sparse Transformer introduces two patterns: local (attending to nearby positions) and strided (attending to positions at fixed intervals), achieving O(n√n) complexity.
